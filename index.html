biography

Lionel Messi (born June 24, 1987, Rosario, Argentina) is an Argentine-born football (soccer) player who received a record-setting eight Ballon d’Or awards as the world’s top male player (2009–12, 2015, 2019, 2021, and 2023). In 2022 he helped Argentina win the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA)’s World Cup.

Will Lionel Messi is retiring his international career

Lionel Messi intends to finish his career with Inter Miami - but has no plans to retire just yet.The eight-time Ballon d'Or winner, 36, joined the Major League Soccer (MLS) club last summer after leaving Paris St-Germain.That followed a glittering career with Barcelona, where he won the Champions League four times, alongside 10 La Liga titles.The 2022 World Cup winner with Argentina has a contract with Inter Miami to 2025 with the option of a further year.
Messi began his career with FC Barcelona and stayed at the Spanish club until joining Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) in 2021.After a troubled two-year stint at the French club, he signed for Major League Soccer’s Inter Miami in June.Messi was part of the Inter Miami team that won the 2023 League Cup and the PSG team that won the 2022-23 French Super Cup. But he etched his legacy in club football during his 21-year association with Barcelona.Over the course of 778 games with the club, Messi scored 672 goals, ahead of Brazil great Pele, who scored 643 goals while playing for Santos. At Barcelona, Messi leads Cesar Rodriguez, who scored 446 goals for the Spanish giants.
